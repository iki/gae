Introducing App Engine for Business

App Engine for Business enables you to build your enterprise applications on the same scalable systems that power Google applications. App Engine for Business provides all the ease of use and flexibility of App Engine with more power to manage enterprise use cases, more capable APIs, straightforward pricing and the SLAs and support you need for business-critical applications.

App Engine for Business is currently in preview. If you’re interested in receiving an invite to try App Engine for Business, please sign up.

  • Central Development Management
  • Support and Reliability that Businesses Require
  • Pricing That Makes Sense
  • Expanded Enterprise Features

For more details, see the App Engine for Business FAQ.

Central Development Management

App Engine has always made it easy to scale your apps. Now we’ve made it easy to securely scale the number of apps you own. The App Engine for Enterprise Administration Console lets you manage all the applications built by anyone in your company, not just the ones you’ve built. And you can use Access Control Lists to determine who has access to the application data, and who can deploy new code. Once more, all of your App Engine for Business applications are secure by default as users must have an account in your Google Apps domain to access them.

App Engine for Business also simplifies the process of creating new applications associated with your domain. You don’t need to create DNS records for each application. You can simply create a subdomain to host all your applications, and they’ll automatically be at the address you specify. For example, a company might use *.apps.example.com for all of its App Engine apps. This feature gives all your new apps the credibility of an easy-to-remember URL on your company’s domain.

Support and Reliability that Businesses Require

In today’s always-on world, businesses expect high availability and reliability, which can be difficult and costly to maintain with existing hosted systems. High reliability at a lower cost is one of the key benefits of moving applications to App Engine and we’re putting our money where our mouth is with a 99.9% Service Level Agreement. In addition, App Engine for Business customers get exclusive access to premium developer support, with guaranteed turnaround times and access to trained support engineers at Google. When you encounter a problem you can’t fix, you can sleep soundly knowing that you can reach someone to help you.

See the App Engine for Business Support page for more details.

Pricing That Makes Sense

App Engine for Business makes it affordable to run your company’s applications in the cloud with simple pricing that doesn’t require quota management or over-provisioning.

Each application costs $8 per user, up to a maximum of $1000, per month.

For $8 per user, the application gets as much processing power as it needs for your intranet users in your Google Apps domain. Each application gets plenty of storage too, but you can also buy more to share across all your applications if you need it. At the end of the month, all of the bills for each application are combined together into a single bill for easy payment - no need to share a company card with the entire development team. And you only ever pay for what you use.

You will also use App Engine for Business to run publicly accessible apps, such as your company's website or service. We’re still working out the details on pricing for that, so stay tuned.

Expanded Enterprise Features

App Engine is a rich platform which provides simple APIs to make use of Google’s many services. App Engine for Business applications can be based on standard Java or Python, and can take advantage of the same APIs as the rest of App Engine. But they also have access to a number of premium services available only to App Engine for Business applications. (Note: premium services, including SSL and SQL, may have an additional cost associated with them.)

Hosted SQL

While many applications can be built on the App Engine Datastore (which uses Google's BigTable database system), we know SQL is the industry standard for the enterprise, so we’ve got you covered. SQL database support on App Engine gives enterprise developers access to the full capabilities of a dedicated relational database, without the headache of managing it. Setup is just as easy as with an App Engine application, and the traditional configuration and maintenance work required to run a database server is taken care of for you.

SSL on Your Domain

App Engine for Business allows you to use SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) on your own domain for all your App Engine applications, not just on the *.appspot.com domain. SSL is a protocol that provides secure communications on the Internet. When used, communication between an employee’s browser and Google’s data centers is encrypted, providing a higher level of data security, particularly when applications are accessed over insecure Internet connections like public WiFi. With App Engine for Business, you gain the security of SSL with the trust of your company’s own domain name, like https://myapp.apps.example.com.

Track our progress on all the features on the App Engine for Business roadmap.
